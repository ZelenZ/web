Lunar New Year Facts

  • What is Lunar New Year?


    Lunar New Year is the celebration of the start of a new year in the Lunar Calender. It starts on the start of the first moon of the lunar calender and ends on the first full moon of the year, 15 days later.


    Britannica's definition of Lunar New Year

  • Where does Lunar New Year originate from?


    Lunar New Year started about 3400 years ago. According to legend, there was once a beast named Nian that lived in the mountains. During most of the year, Nian would stay up in the mountains and hunt food up there. However, near the end of winter, food would become scarce and Nian would go down mountain into the villages to consume livestock, crops and children. However, it was discovered that loud noises, fire and the color red scared Nian. Afterwhich, Nian never came back to the villages again and the villagers would celebrate each year with Lunar New Year.


    Legend of Nian

  • How is Lunar New Year celebrated?


    At the start of the Lunar New Year, houses are cleaned to clear them of air from the old year. There are ritual sacrifices of food and paper icons. Scrolls with lucky messages printed on are hung on doors. People set off firecrackers to scare off spirits. Adults give money in red envelopes to children. The extended family would gather to feast, eating long noodles and round dumplings.
